Understanding Static Websites

What they are & what they aren't

What static websites CAN do

  • Display your business info — services, rooms, rates, gallery, location, contact details
  • Look professional and build trust with potential guests or clients
  • Link to your WhatsApp, social media, or Google Maps
  • Load extremely fast because there's no database or server processing
  • Be hosted for free or very cheap on platforms like Netlify, Vercel, or GitHub Pages
  • Showcase image galleries, embedded videos, and PDF menus/brochures

What static websites CANNOT do

  • Process online bookings or payments — you'll need a third-party link (e.g. Bookings.com, WhatsApp form)
  • Have a user login system, dashboard, or admin panel
  • Dynamically update content — changes require me to edit the code and re-upload
  • Run a blog with comments, categories, or search — they're not CMS-based
  • Collect form submissions without a third-party service (e.g. Formspree, Web3Forms)
  • Scale into a full web app — they're meant to be simple, informative pages
